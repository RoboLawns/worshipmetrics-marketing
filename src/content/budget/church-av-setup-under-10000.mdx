## Four-Camera PTZ System

Four cameras (mix of AVKANS, SmartAV BX30N, or similar models, around $2,000 total) positioned for comprehensive coverage. Wide on the entire stage. Medium on the pastor. Medium on worship leader. Tight on audience or specific worship moments. You have every angle covered simultaneously.

Your switcher operator isn't scrambling to frame shots. They're choosing from four perfect angles and switching between them intentionally. Your camera work is sophisticated because you have the tools and time to frame properly.

All four cameras connect to your PoE network. One cable per camera carries power and video. They're all controlled via joysticks at your switcher position.

## ATEM Mini Extreme

The ATEM Mini Extreme (around $750) is the most advanced switcher in the Mini series. Eight HDMI inputs (you can connect all four cameras plus ProPresenter, backup camera, video playback, and graphics). Two independent program outputs. Full internal mixing capabilities.

More importantly, the Mini Extreme gives you enough complexity to handle sophisticated productions without becoming overwhelming. You can create nested scenes, complex transitions, and multi-layer effects if you want them. But you can also keep it simple if you prefer.

## Allen & Heath SQ-5 Professional Console

The SQ-5 (around $3,500) is a professional digital mixing console designed for venues. It's not overkill for a church—it's exactly right for a service that's being mixed for both in-room PA and broadcast simultaneously.

The SQ-5 has 40 channels of mixing capability, motorized faders, touchscreen interface, and separate outputs for Main PA, Monitor Mix, Streaming Mix, and Recording Mix. You're mixing professional-grade audio infrastructure.

Most importantly, the SQ-5 supports Dante networking, which means audio can flow across your network alongside video. Your audio engineer doesn't need a rat's nest of analog cables running across your facility. Everything is network-based, which is more reliable and more flexible.

## Hardware Encoding: Magewell Ultra Encode HDMI

The Magewell Ultra Encode (around $800) is a dedicated hardware encoder that takes your ATEM Mini Extreme's HDMI output and encodes it to H.264 at broadcast quality. Unlike software encoders, the Magewell is a specialized device that handles encoding as its only job.

If your PC crashes, your stream doesn't drop. If OBS has a driver issue, your Magewell keeps streaming. You're getting streaming that's independent from your PC, which means it's more resilient and more reliable.

The Magewell outputs simultaneously to YouTube, Facebook, and your church website (or any other streaming platform). Each platform gets optimized bitrate and quality settings automatically.

## vMix Production Software

vMix (around $800 for a license) is production software designed for broadcast. It's more powerful than OBS and more intuitive than command-line encoding tools. vMix handles multiple input sources, graphics, automation, and multi-platform streaming from a single interface.

More importantly, vMix is NDI-native, which means it integrates beautifully with your network infrastructure. Your cameras send video over the network, vMix receives NDI feeds natively, and you're operating a modern, integrated system.

vMix also handles advanced features like live chat overlay, chroma key (green screen), and streaming to multiple platforms with different graphics and aspect ratios for each. You're not limited to "same graphics for YouTube and Facebook." You can optimize each platform independently.

## Training and Team Structure

You now have a professional production team:

- Production Director (oversees the entire operation, coordinates between technical and pastoral staff)
- Switcher Operator (operates cameras and switcher)
- Audio Engineer (manages mixing for both PA and streaming)
- Graphics Operator (creates lower-thirds, full screens, and graphics)
- Streaming Technical Director (monitors encoding, internet, and troubleshoots streaming issues)

These might be four people or three or even two people juggling roles. But the roles are distinct and the responsibilities are clear. This is a coordinated technical operation, not volunteer chaos.

## Post-Production and Content Creation

Your post-production workflow is sophisticated:

- Monday: Archive ISO recordings are backed up to network storage
- Tuesday: Editor reviews and pulls highlight moments
- Wednesday: Create 5-10 social media clips (30 seconds to 2 minutes each)
- Thursday-Friday: Create longer-form content (full sermon, worship package, team interview)
- Weekly: Post content to YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, church website, newsletter

You're not just streaming a Sunday service. You're creating a week's worth of content that keeps your audience engaged. Your media ministry becomes strategically important to your overall ministry.
