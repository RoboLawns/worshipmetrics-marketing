Restream and Castr are both multistreaming platforms that distribute your single OBS stream to YouTube, Facebook, and other platforms simultaneously. For churches evaluating which to use, the choice comes down to features, price, reliability, and ease of use. Both are solid choices; the right one depends on your church's specific needs and preferences.

## Pricing Comparison

Restream offers a free tier supporting two simultaneous destinations (YouTube and Facebook), with limited features. Paid tiers start at $19/month for unlimited destinations and advanced features.

Castr's free tier is more restrictive—it only supports one destination. Paid plans start at $39/month for two simultaneous destinations. For churches wanting just YouTube and Facebook, Restream's free tier is more generous. Castr forces you to pay if you want multistreaming.

If you value free functionality, Restream wins. If you're willing to pay for better features, the pricing difference is modest ($19 vs. $39/month), and you'd evaluate based on features rather than cost.

## Platform Support

Restream supports a vast platform list: YouTube, Facebook, Twitch, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, and dozens of others. If your church streams to unusual platforms (a niche streaming service, corporate video platform, etc.), Restream likely supports it.

Castr supports the major platforms (YouTube, Facebook, Twitch) and several others, but the list is shorter. For most churches streaming to YouTube and Facebook, both platforms support everything you need.

If your church might expand to additional platforms in the future (streaming to LinkedIn for business members, Twitch for gaming community, etc.), Restream's broader platform list is an advantage.

## Feature Set

Restream includes unified chat (see comments from all platforms in one inbox), per-platform analytics (viewer count per platform), and clip creation tools (automatically clip moments from your stream).

Castr includes similar features but positions them differently. Castr emphasizes reliability and latency optimization. Their tagline is around providing lower-latency streams than competitors.

For churches, unified chat and analytics are nice-to-haves but not critical. Latency (the delay between your stream starting and viewers receiving it) typically ranges from 10–30 seconds on both platforms, which is acceptable for church streams. Restream and Castr perform similarly in most use cases.

## Interface and Ease of Use

Restream's interface is clean and intuitive. Adding destinations, configuring metadata, and monitoring streams is straightforward. New users can set it up within 10–15 minutes.

Castr's interface is also clean, though slightly more feature-dense. Configuration is more granular, which is powerful but requires more setup. New users might take 20–30 minutes to configure.

For churches with volunteer tech directors who prefer simplicity, Restream feels easier. For churches with dedicated technical staff who want fine-grained control, Castr offers more customization.

## Reliability

Both platforms are reliable. Restream is the larger platform (higher user base), so it has proven track record across thousands of simultaneous streams. Castr is newer but offers similar reliability with fewer users. Real-world reliability differences are minimal—both are suitable for church use.

Platform-specific failures (where one destination stops receiving video while others work) occur occasionally on both, and both handle them similarly. You'd see it in their dashboard and can re-authorize the platform.

## Latency

Castr emphasizes low-latency streaming. Their marketing claims 2–5 second latency (viewer sees your stream almost live). Restream's latency is typically 10–30 seconds. For interactive churches (where chat engagement is real-time), Castr's lower latency is better.

In practice, most church streams don't require ultra-low latency. Viewers don't mind a 20-second delay. The difference matters more if you're doing live Q&A or real-time chat moderation during service (less common in churches).

## Analytics and Reporting

Restream provides detailed analytics: viewer count over time, platform-specific metrics, viewer retention, and geographic data. This is valuable for understanding your audience.

Castr provides basic analytics but less detailed. If data-driven insights into your audience are important, Restream is superior.

## Community and Support

Restream has a larger community and more third-party integrations (plugins, webhooks, API integrations). If you want to do advanced things (automatically post clips to Twitter, trigger events in other software), Restream has more options.

Castr's community is smaller but responsive. Support is good, though less third-party ecosystem.

## Customer Support

Restream provides email support and knowledge base. Castr includes email support and live chat support (faster response). For urgent issues, Castr's live chat is an advantage.

## When to Choose Restream

Choose Restream if you want the simplest, most generous free tier. Free Restream works great for YouTube and Facebook.

Choose Restream if you plan to stream to multiple platforms (more than YouTube and Facebook). Restream's broader platform list is an advantage.

Choose Restream if analytics and viewer insights are important. Restream's reporting is more detailed.

Choose Restream if you want unified chat monitoring. Restream's chat aggregation is clean and useful.

## When to Choose Castr

Choose Castr if ultra-low latency is critical. Castr's 2–5 second latency enables real-time interaction you can't get with Restream.

Choose Castr if you prefer more granular, advanced control over stream settings. Castr's configuration is more detailed.

Choose Castr if live chat support is important. Castr includes it; Restream doesn't.

Choose Castr if you're willing to pay for better features from the start. Castr's paid plans include advanced features Restream reserves for higher tiers.

## The Honest Comparison

Both are excellent multistreaming platforms suitable for churches. Restream is the more popular choice for churches because of the generous free tier and simplicity. Castr is a solid alternative if you prioritize latency, advanced control, or prefer the paid tier approach.

For most churches' first multistreaming platform, Restream is the practical choice. It's free to start, easy to set up, and handles YouTube and Facebook with no configuration. If your church later needs advanced features or additional platforms, you can stay with Restream or migrate to Castr.

Test both free plans and see which feels more comfortable for your team. The choice will become obvious within a few minutes of setup.
