## Introduction

The Tenveo VHD20U is a budget-friendly 20x zoom PTZ camera that emphasizes HDMI output for integration with switchers and capture devices. Unlike the VHD30U which offers both USB and HDMI, the VHD20U is optimized for HDMI connectivity, making it an excellent choice for churches using hardware switchers like ATEM Mini or Roland V-Mixer.

The 20x optical zoom is sufficient for most church spaces, allowing you to frame a distant speaker or worship team member with clarity from a fixed camera position. This guide covers unboxing, HDMI connection, initial configuration, and creating your first presets for production use.

## What Comes in the Box

The VHD20U arrives with the camera unit, HDMI cable, power adapter, control remote, and documentation. Unlike USB cameras that require driver installation, the VHD20U connects directly to your switcher or capture device via HDMI with no additional software needed.

The simplicity of HDMI connection means minimal setup is required before you can see video. If you have ever connected a camera or laptop to a projector via HDMI, you already understand the basic concept.

## Connect Power and Verify Operation

Connect the power adapter to the camera and plug it into an outlet. The camera boots up, which takes a few seconds. You should see a power indicator light confirming the camera is on.

If the camera does not power on, check that the power outlet is functioning by plugging in another device. Also verify that the power adapter is fully connected to both the camera and the wall outlet.

## Connect HDMI to Your Switcher or Capture Device

Connect an HDMI cable from the camera to an available input on your switcher (ATEM Mini, Roland V-Mixer, etc.) or to an HDMI capture device on your production computer. Most HDMI cables are standard, but verify that your cable is rated for HDMI video (not just HDMI audio).

Once connected, select the appropriate input on your switcher or capture device. You should see video from the camera appearing in your preview or on your monitor. If you do not see video, check the HDMI cable connection at both the camera and the switcher. Make sure the input is actually selected on the switcher.

## Verify Video Quality

Check that the video quality looks clean and clear, with no artifacts, pixelation, or color issues. The 20x zoom should deliver sharp video whether the camera is zoomed in or out. If the video appears degraded, check the HDMI cable for damage or poor connection.

Test the HDMI cable by trying a different cable if possible, or by moving the camera to a different location temporarily to rule out cable damage. HDMI cables rarely fail, but a physically damaged cable can result in poor video quality.

## Familiarize Yourself with the Remote Control

The VHD20U ships with a control remote that allows you to zoom, pan, tilt, and recall presets. Spend a few moments learning the remote buttons so you can operate the camera confidently during service.

The remote typically has these controls:

Zoom in and zoom out: These adjust the 20x optical zoom.

Pan and tilt: These move the camera horizontally and vertically.

Preset buttons: These save and recall camera positions.

Power or standby: Some remotes include a power control.

Menu or settings: This navigates the camera's configuration options.

Confirm that the remote is working by using it to zoom or pan the camera. You should see the camera responding immediately to remote commands.

## Create Your First Service Presets

Using the remote control, position the camera at your key service moments and save presets. Most churches need three to four presets: a wide stage view, a pulpit or speaker position, a worship team position, and possibly an announcements position.

Move the camera to the desired position using the joystick or pan/tilt buttons. Adjust zoom to the appropriate level. Once the framing looks right, use the Preset Save function on the remote. Your documentation will specify the exact button sequence, but typically you press a Menu or Settings button, navigate to Presets, select a preset number, and press Save.

Test the preset by moving to a different position and recalling the preset. The camera should return to exactly the saved position and zoom level.

## Document Your Preset Configuration

Create a simple reference sheet showing your presets:

Preset 1: Wide — Full stage, entire team visible
Preset 2: Pulpit — Speaker close-up
Preset 3: Worship — Worship team tight shot
Preset 4: Announcements — Pulpit or stage position for announcements

Print this reference and tape it near your camera remote or keep it accessible during service. This eliminates confusion about which preset to use.

## Test Your HDMI Setup Before Service

Run a full service rehearsal using the VHD20U connected to your switcher. Verify that video remains stable throughout the broadcast, that all presets recall correctly, and that your switcher handles the camera input reliably.

If video quality degrades during the rehearsal, check for HDMI cable issues or interference from other equipment. If presets do not recall reliably, verify that you saved them correctly.

## Configure Default Camera Position

Set the camera to a sensible default position and zoom level when it powers on. For most churches, this is a slightly zoomed-in view that shows good context without excessive zoom. This saves time at startup because the camera powers on ready to use.

## Verify Remote Battery

Confirm that the remote has fresh batteries so it operates reliably during service. Replace batteries at the start of each month or when you notice the remote becoming sluggish. A working remote is essential for preset operation.

## Conclusion

Tenveo VHD20U setup is straightforward because HDMI connectivity requires no drivers or complex configuration. Once you connect the HDMI cable and confirm video appears on your switcher, the camera is ready for preset configuration and service use.

The simplicity of HDMI integration combined with the 20x zoom makes the VHD20U an affordable and practical choice for churches using hardware switchers. Test your complete HDMI setup during a service rehearsal before going live to ensure all components work together reliably.
