The OBSBOT Tiny 4K uses USB-C for both power and video data transmission. While USB video devices are generally plug-and-play, understanding USB-C configuration, driver installation, and power management ensures reliable operation. This guide covers all aspects of USB-C connectivity.

## USB-C Cable Quality and Power Delivery

The Tiny 4K requires a quality USB-C cable. While it might work with any USB-C cable, higher-quality cables with better shielding ensure stable power and data transmission. Use the cable included with the camera, or purchase quality USB-C cables from reputable manufacturers.

For power delivery, the Tiny 4K requires several watts of power. Use a dedicated power adapter rather than relying on your computer's USB ports. A dedicated USB power adapter with at least 2 amps output provides reliable power.

If you use a powered USB hub, verify that it provides adequate power output. Some hubs are underpowered and can't reliably power the camera.

## Connecting to Windows Computers

Connect the USB-C cable from the Tiny 4K to a USB-C port on your Windows computer. Windows will automatically search for and install drivers. You might see a notification in the taskbar showing that the device is being installed.

Once the installation completes, the camera will appear in your device manager under "Imaging Devices" or "Cameras." It will also be available in OBS, vMix, or other applications that access video devices.

If Windows doesn't automatically install drivers, visit the OBSBOT website and download the latest USB drivers for Windows. Install them manually and reconnect the camera.

## Connecting to Mac Computers

Connect the USB-C cable and Mac should recognize the camera immediately. No driver installation is required—Macs have built-in support for standard USB video devices.

The camera will appear in System Preferences under USB, and will be available in OBS, Zoom, or any other application that accesses video devices.

If the camera doesn't appear, try a different USB-C port on your Mac. Some ports have better power delivery or data transfer characteristics.

## Identifying the Camera in Device Lists

In applications like OBS, the Tiny 4K might appear in the video device list as "OBSBOT" or "Tiny 4K" or with a generic description like "USB Video Device." If you're unsure which device is the camera, disconnect it and look at the list—the camera's entry will disappear. Reconnect it to confirm.

In system settings and device managers, the camera typically appears as an imaging device. Windows might show it as "OBSBOT Camera" or a similar descriptor.

## USB Power Limitations and Alternatives

Standard USB ports provide up to 500 mA (0.5 amps) at 5 volts. USB 3.0 and USB-C ports provide up to 900 mA (0.9 amps). The Tiny 4K requires more power than standard USB, so a dedicated power adapter is necessary.

USB Power Delivery (USB-PD) is a specification that allows USB-C cables to carry higher power. If your power adapter supports USB-PD, it can deliver significantly more power (up to 100 watts in some cases). This provides the most reliable power delivery.

For best results, use a USB-PD power adapter that provides at least 30 watts output. This ensures the camera has plenty of power even during intensive operation.

## Troubleshooting USB Connection Issues

If the camera doesn't appear in your computer's device list, try a different USB-C cable. Cables can be faulty or not support the full feature set needed for video transmission.

Try a different USB-C port on your computer. Some ports have better power or data characteristics. Rear ports on desktop computers are sometimes more reliable than front ports.

If the camera appears in device manager but doesn't work in applications, restart the applications. Sometimes they need to refresh the list of available devices.

If you're using a USB-C hub, try connecting directly to your computer's USB-C port instead. Some hubs don't properly support video data transmission.

## Multi-Computer Scenarios

You can use the Tiny 4K with multiple computers, but only one at a time. Disconnect from one computer before connecting to another.

If you frequently switch between computers, establish a consistent connection procedure. Always use the same USB port if possible, and restart applications after connecting to a new computer.

## USB Bandwidth and Cable Length

USB 3.0 and USB 3.1 support bandwidth up to 5 Gbps and 10 Gbps respectively, more than adequate for 4K video. USB 2.0 is theoretically possible for video, but limited to 480 Mbps. Verify your port supports at least USB 3.0.

USB-C cables work reliably up to 10 feet. Beyond that distance, use an active USB-C repeater or extender. Passive extensions can degrade signal quality.

## Power Management

The Tiny 4K consumes significant power only during video transmission. When the camera is idle or just in standby, power consumption is minimal. For continuous operation during long services, ensure your power source is reliable.

If you're using a laptop's battery for extended operation, the battery will drain faster with the camera connected. Consider having the laptop plugged in during services.

## Driver Updates

Periodically check the OBSBOT website for driver updates. While the Tiny 4K works with generic USB video drivers, manufacturer-specific drivers sometimes provide better compatibility or additional features.

When installing driver updates, follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully. Some updates require disconnecting the camera before installation, while others can be installed with the camera connected.

## Firmware Updates

Some OBSBOT models allow firmware updates through a desktop application or through the OBSBOT app. Check for firmware updates periodically. Updated firmware sometimes fixes issues or adds new features.

Never interrupt a firmware update. Let it complete fully, even if it takes several minutes. Interrupting a firmware update can corrupt the camera's software.

## USB Monitoring Software

If you experience USB connection issues, use USB monitoring software to see what's happening at the device level. Software like USB Analyzer or USBlyzer can show data transfer rates and errors.

For troubleshooting most issues, the basic steps (try different cables, different ports, restart applications) are usually sufficient without needing advanced monitoring tools.

## Conclusion

USB-C connectivity on the OBSBOT Tiny 4K is straightforward for most users. Using quality cables, adequate power, and updated drivers ensures reliable operation. When issues occur, systematic troubleshooting usually identifies and resolves them quickly. Once properly configured, the Tiny 4K provides years of reliable service without requiring complex technical management.
