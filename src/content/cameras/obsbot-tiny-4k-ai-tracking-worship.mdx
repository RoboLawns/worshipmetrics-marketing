The OBSBOT Tiny 4K's AI tracking capability combined with 4K video quality creates stunningly detailed video of your worship services. The camera automatically follows speakers and musicians, providing dynamic, engaging footage without requiring a camera operator. This guide covers optimizing tracking for worship environments.

## How AI Tracking Works on the Tiny 4K

Unlike traditional PTZ cameras with motorized pan-tilt mechanisms, the Tiny 4K uses digital image processing to crop and zoom within the 4K frame, effectively following people through panning and zooming. This creates smooth tracking without mechanical motors.

The tracking processes faces and bodies in the frame, determining the most prominent person to follow. As speakers move, the camera smoothly adjusts to keep them framed nicely.

## Positioning for Effective Tracking

Position the Tiny 4K where it can see your speakers or primary focus area clearly. The camera doesn't need an extremely wide view—it can track people through panning within its field of view.

Mount the camera at a comfortable eye level or slightly above where your speakers stand. This creates natural-looking camera angles even when the tracking is active.

Ensure the camera has adequate lighting. Faces and bodies are easiest to track in well-lit environments. Use your normal stage lighting—you don't need special lighting for tracking to work.

## Configuring Tracking Sensitivity

Use the OBSBOT app to access tracking settings. Adjust tracking sensitivity to match your worship style. For worship with relatively stationary speakers, moderate sensitivity works well. For energetic music or movement-heavy presentations, higher sensitivity keeps up better.

Test your sensitivity settings during rehearsal with your actual speakers. Watch the resulting video in OBS to see how the tracking looks. Make adjustments until the tracking feels smooth and natural.

## Handling Multiple People

The Tiny 4K tracks the most prominent person in view. If two speakers are on stage, it typically follows the one closest to the camera or most centered in the frame. As speakers move, the tracking might shift focus between them.

For multiple speakers, position them so one is clearly the primary focus for each segment. For example, position the pastor center stage during the sermon so tracking focuses on them. Move the worship leader to the center during worship so they become the focus.

## Managing Transitions Between Speakers

Plan smooth transitions between different speakers or segments. Have speakers move gradually into the primary focus area rather than appearing suddenly. This allows the camera to smoothly transition its tracking rather than jumping suddenly.

During rehearsal, observe how the tracking handles transitions between your different speakers. Make adjustments to positioning if needed.

## Manual Control During Automatic Tracking

While tracking is active, you maintain manual control through the OBSBOT app. You can pan, tilt, or zoom manually at any time, overriding the automatic tracking.

This hybrid approach gives you automatic camera work most of the time, with the flexibility to manually adjust when needed. For example, you might disable tracking to show a wide view of the entire stage, then re-enable it to follow a speaker.

## Using Presets for Non-Tracking Scenes

Create presets for scenes that don't use tracking. For example, a wide opening shot of your entire stage, or a preset for transitions between segments. You can toggle between tracking and these static presets throughout your service.

This combination of automatic tracking and manual preset control provides a complete workflow that feels dynamic and intentional.

## Optimizing Tracking for 4K

The 4K resolution of the Tiny 4K is excellent for tracking. The high detail makes facial expressions and hand movements clear and engaging, even when the camera is following the speaker at distance.

The 4K quality is especially valuable when viewers are watching on large screens or when you want to record high-quality archive footage. Viewers notice and appreciate the crystal-clear detail.

## Handling Stage Lighting Challenges

Different stage lighting setups can affect tracking performance. Very dim lighting or overly bright spotlights can temporarily confuse the tracking algorithm.

If you encounter tracking issues, review your stage lighting. Adjust lights to provide even illumination without harsh shadows or extreme bright spots. This improves both tracking performance and the visual quality of your broadcast.

## Integrating Tracking with Your Broadcast

In OBS, the Tiny 4K appears as a regular video source. The tracking happens on the camera itself, not in OBS. This means the tracking is visible in your broadcast output without any special OBS configuration.

You can apply OBS effects, overlays, or titles over the tracked video. The tracking continues smoothly behind any graphics you add.

## Recording AI-Tracked Video

When you record the Tiny 4K's output in OBS, the recording includes all the AI tracking movements. This creates very dynamic, professional-looking footage when you play it back or edit it later.

Archive recordings made this way are excellent for creating highlight reels or editing together special service compilations.

## Fine-Tuning During Service

Before your service starts, test the tracking with your actual speakers in your actual stage setup. If anything seems off, make quick adjustments to sensitivity or positioning.

Keep the OBSBOT app open during your broadcast so you can make real-time adjustments if needed. Most churches find they don't need to adjust during the service once they've properly configured tracking beforehand.

## Troubleshooting Tracking Issues

If tracking seems jerky or unstable, reduce the sensitivity. Very high sensitivity can cause the camera to jump between targets erratically.

If tracking doesn't activate, verify that tracking is enabled in the OBSBOT app. Some app versions require you to explicitly enable tracking mode.

If the camera tracks the wrong person, reposition your speakers or move the person you want to track more toward the center of the stage.

The OBSBOT Tiny 4K's AI tracking transforms your worship broadcasts. The automatic speaker following provides dynamic, engaging video that viewers prefer to watch. Combined with 4K resolution, the Tiny 4K delivers professional quality at an affordable price point. Your church broadcasts will benefit from this combination of technology and flexibility.
