## Introduction

The Tenveo VHD30U is an affordable PTZ camera with impressive 30x optical zoom in a compact, budget-friendly package. Unlike larger professional PTZ cameras, the VHD30U connects directly to your production computer via USB, appearing as a webcam source in OBS and other software. It also supports HDMI output for routing to switchers or capture devices.

For churches with limited budgets that still want zoom capability and remote camera control, the VHD30U offers professional zoom at a consumer-friendly price point. This guide covers unboxing, USB and HDMI connection, initial camera control, and setting up your first presets so you can move from setup to production use within an hour.

## What Comes in the Box

The VHD30U arrives with the camera unit, a USB cable, an HDMI cable, a power adapter, control remote (on some models), and documentation. Unlike the AVKANS models which use network infrastructure, the Tenveo connects directly to your production computer via USB or to your switcher via HDMI.

The USB connection makes setup straightforward: plug in the USB cable and the camera appears as a device to your production software. The HDMI output allows you to route video to a separate switcher or capture device if you prefer not to use USB.

## Connect Power and Initial Setup

Connect the power adapter to the camera and plug it into an outlet. The camera should power up and show a boot-up indicator (usually a light). Wait for the boot sequence to complete before attempting to use the camera.

If you are using the camera in a fixed location like mounted above your stage, you can leave the power connected at all times. The camera does not consume much power when idle, and this ensures it is always ready for use.

## Connect USB to Your Production Computer

Connect the included USB cable from the camera to your production computer. The operating system should automatically recognize the camera as a USB device. On Windows, you may see a driver installation notification. Allow the installation to complete.

Once the USB driver is installed, the camera appears in your system as a USB video device, available to any software that can use USB cameras. This includes OBS, Zoom, Teams, or any other application that accepts video input.

Test the USB connection by opening a video application and selecting the Tenveo camera as the video source. You should see a live video preview from the camera. If you do not see video, try disconnecting and reconnecting the USB cable, or restart the video application.

## Configure Camera Settings Using the Control Remote

Most Tenveo cameras ship with a control remote that allows you to zoom, pan, tilt, and move through settings. If your camera includes a remote, familiarize yourself with its controls. The remote usually has buttons for:

Zoom in and zoom out: These control the 30x optical zoom. Practice using the zoom to frame distant objects.

Pan and tilt: These move the camera horizontally and vertically. Use them to position the camera for different shots.

Preset buttons: These save and recall camera positions. You will use these to save your scene positions.

Settings: This navigates the camera's menu system for configuration options.

Spend a few minutes becoming comfortable with the remote before service. Different operators may have different preferences, so a brief practice session prevents confusion during Sunday operation.

## Alternative: Control via USB Software

If your camera did not ship with a physical remote, or if you prefer software-based control, you may be able to control the camera through the USB connection using control software. Some Tenveo models include proprietary control software that allows pan, tilt, zoom, and preset management through your production computer.

Check your documentation to see if control software is available for your model. If it is, install it and use it to test camera control before service.

## Create Initial Presets for Your Service

Using the remote or control software, position the camera at your key service moments and save presets. Most churches need three to five presets: a wide stage view, a speaker or pulpit position, a worship team position, and possibly special positions for announcements or prayer moments.

Move the camera to the desired position, adjust zoom as needed, and press the preset save button on the remote. The camera stores the position and number. Test each preset by moving to a different position and then recalling the preset—the camera should return to the exact saved position.

Document your preset numbers and what each one is used for. Create a simple reference like:

Preset 1: Wide stage, full team visible
Preset 2: Pulpit speaker close-up
Preset 3: Worship team or band
Preset 4: Announcements position

This documentation helps your operator quickly identify which preset to use at each moment.

## Test USB Video Quality in Your Production Software

Open OBS or your production software and add the Tenveo as a Video Capture Device source. The camera should appear in your available devices. Select it and confirm that video appears in the preview. The video quality should be clean and sharp, with no artifacts or flickering.

If the video appears pixelated or has compression artifacts, check your USB cable connection. Ensure the cable is fully seated and of good quality. A loose or damaged USB cable can result in video quality degradation.

Test the camera at different zoom levels to confirm that quality remains good whether zoomed in or out. The 30x zoom should deliver clear video at all zoom positions.

## Optional: Configure HDMI Output

If you want to route video to an HDMI switcher or capture device instead of using USB, connect an HDMI cable from the camera to your device. The camera automatically outputs video on HDMI while also supporting USB. You can use either output, or both simultaneously if your workflow requires it.

Test the HDMI connection by viewing video on your switcher or capture device. If you see no video, check the HDMI cable connection and confirm your device is set to the correct input port.

## Set Your Default Zoom Level

By default, the camera starts at a particular zoom level each time it powers on. If you prefer the camera to start zoomed in or zoomed out, check the camera settings to set your preferred default zoom. This can save time at startup if your primary shot is at a specific zoom level.

## Document Your Setup for Future Reference

Document the USB port where the camera is connected, your preset configuration, any control software you installed, and any custom settings you have configured. This documentation becomes invaluable if you need to troubleshoot or if a volunteer needs to understand your setup.

Store this documentation in an accessible shared location, not just on one person's computer.

## Conclusion

The Tenveo VHD30U setup is straightforward because it connects directly to your production computer without complex networking. The 30x zoom capability and preset system make it a capable and affordable option for churches that want PTZ functionality without the cost and complexity of larger systems.

Test your complete USB setup during a service rehearsal before going live. This ensures that the camera remains connected during real-world network usage and that your operator is comfortable using the preset system.
