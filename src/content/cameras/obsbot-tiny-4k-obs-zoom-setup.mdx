The OBSBOT Tiny 4K works with virtually any software that accepts USB video devices, making it incredibly versatile for churches. Use it in OBS for streaming, in Zoom for hybrid services, in Teams for staff meetings, or in multiple applications simultaneously. This guide covers integration with the most common platforms.

## USB Video Device Compatibility

The Tiny 4K appears as a standard USB video device to your computer, making it compatible with any software that can access video. Windows and Mac automatically recognize it when you plug in the USB cable.

Once the camera is connected and recognized, any application that has access to video devices can use it. This universal compatibility is one of the camera's biggest strengths.

## Using the Tiny 4K in OBS Studio

Connect the camera to your broadcast computer via USB-C. In OBS, add a new source and select "Video Capture Device." The OBSBOT Tiny 4K should appear in the dropdown list.

Select it and click OK. Live 4K video from the camera will appear in your scene. The high resolution will make the image appear very large—you might need to zoom out in OBS to see the entire frame, or crop it to the size you need.

Use the OBSBOT app to control tracking, adjust framing, and recall presets while OBS handles the streaming or recording.

## Using the Tiny 4K in Zoom for Hybrid Services

If your church broadcasts to Zoom for remote attendees, the Tiny 4K provides excellent video quality. In Zoom settings, select the OBSBOT Tiny 4K as your camera device. Zoom will use the camera automatically for all video.

The AI tracking will follow your pastor as they move around, creating engaging video for remote participants. Zoom handles the 4K video beautifully, providing high-quality video to attendees even on the host's video feed.

For Zoom, the 4K resolution provides noticeably better quality than standard 1080p cameras, especially when attendees are watching on larger screens.

## Using the Tiny 4K in Microsoft Teams

Connect the camera and go to Teams Settings. In Devices, select the OBSBOT Tiny 4K as your camera. Teams will use the camera for all video meetings.

The 4K resolution provides excellent video quality for Teams meetings. Staff meetings, board meetings, and other gatherings using Teams will benefit from the crisp, detailed video.

Use the OBSBOT app to adjust framing and tracking while you're in Teams meetings. The app works independently of Teams, so you can control the camera without disrupting your meeting.

## Multi-Application Setup

You can use the Tiny 4K in multiple applications if your computer supports it. For example, you might stream to YouTube using OBS while simultaneously providing video to Zoom for a hybrid service.

However, note that most operating systems don't allow the same camera to be accessed by multiple applications simultaneously. You'll typically need to choose one primary application that "owns" the camera, or use virtual camera software to split the feed.

## Using OBS Virtual Camera with Other Applications

OBS has a virtual camera feature that allows you to use OBS as the video source for other applications. This lets you apply OBS effects, overlays, or multiple camera sources before sending the video to Zoom, Teams, or other applications.

To use this workflow: Set up your Tiny 4K in OBS with the framing and effects you want. Enable the OBS Virtual Camera in Tools menu. In Zoom or Teams, select the OBS Virtual Camera as your video device instead of the physical camera.

## Bandwidth Considerations for Remote Streaming

Streaming 4K video over the internet requires substantial bandwidth. Most internet connections can't sustain a full 4K stream reliably. Consider these options:

Stream 4K locally to your church's network while providing a lower-resolution stream to internet platforms like YouTube or Facebook. This gives your on-site viewers excellent quality while being practical for internet delivery.

Reduce the streaming resolution for internet services. OBS can downscale the 4K capture to 1080p for streaming while recording the full 4K locally. This provides the best of both worlds.

## Recording 4K from Zoom or Teams

If you want to record your Zoom or Teams meetings at high quality, use the OBSBOT app to adjust framing and then let the recording capture the 4K video directly from the Tiny 4K. Alternatively, use OBS to capture and record the video independently.

Recording directly from the camera bypasses any compression that Zoom or Teams might apply, giving you the highest quality archive.

## Setup Optimization Tips

Position the Tiny 4K so it captures your primary speaker or stage area. The AI tracking will follow movement within that area.

Ensure adequate lighting. Unlike some cameras, the Tiny 4K performs best with ample stage or ambient lighting. Very dim environments can result in grainy video.

Test the camera with your actual speakers or content before your first live use. Different people and different stage setups might require different tracking sensitivity settings.

## Troubleshooting Camera Recognition

If your computer doesn't recognize the Tiny 4K, ensure the USB-C cable is fully inserted on both ends. Try a different USB port.

If you're connecting through a USB hub, try connecting directly to a port on your computer instead. Some hubs don't provide adequate power for the camera.

If the camera appears in your system but applications don't recognize it, restart the applications after connecting the camera.

## Performance Optimization

4K video is processor-intensive. If your computer struggles with 4K in OBS, reduce the resolution to 1080p in your OBS settings. You can still use the 4K camera, but OBS will downscale it.

Close other applications before streaming to free up processing power. Reduce OBS filters and effects if performance is sluggish.

## Integration with Other USB Cameras

If you want to use the Tiny 4K alongside other USB cameras for multi-camera setups, you can add multiple cameras in OBS. However, be aware that multiple USB cameras consume processor resources. Most computers can handle two USB cameras simultaneously, but performance might suffer with three or more.

## Conclusion

The OBSBOT Tiny 4K's universal USB compatibility makes it adaptable to nearly any workflow. Whether you're streaming to OBS, hosting hybrid services in Zoom, conducting Teams meetings, or all of the above, the Tiny 4K delivers professional 4K quality with automatic AI tracking. Your church can provide excellent video quality across all these platforms without needing different cameras for different purposes.
