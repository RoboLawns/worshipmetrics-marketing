Multistreaming — sending your church service simultaneously to YouTube, Facebook, and other platforms — requires either complex software configuration or a dedicated multistreaming service. Restream and Castr are the dominant platforms for churches choosing the service route. Both handle the complexity of simultaneously encoding and distributing to multiple platforms, but they differ in pricing, features, and operational experience.

## Restream.io Overview

Restream is the most widely adopted multistreaming platform in churches. It costs around $200-600 annually depending on feature tier, but offers a free tier that handles basic simultaneous streaming.

The value proposition is straightforward: connect your OBS or encoder to Restream, and Restream handles sending your stream to YouTube, Facebook, Twitch, LinkedIn, and other platforms simultaneously. You manage one stream going to Restream; Restream distributes it.

The free tier is surprisingly capable. You get simultaneous streaming to multiple platforms, basic chat management, and fundamental analytics. For churches just exploring multistreaming, the free tier is genuinely useful and costs nothing.

The paid tiers add features like advanced chat moderation, analytics depth, custom branding, and API access. For most churches, the free tier or basic paid tier is sufficient.

The community documentation is extensive. Church tech directors constantly share Restream configurations on YouTube and in forums. If you need help setting up Restream with OBS, you'll find multiple tutorials specifically addressing church use cases. This peer documentation is genuinely valuable.

The interface is intuitive. Connecting to Restream is straightforward — paste your streaming key into OBS, and Restream handles the rest. The setup process is simple enough that volunteers can manage it.

However, Restream depends on your internet upload speed. If your church internet connection has limited upload bandwidth, Restream's simultaneous streaming to multiple platforms might overwhelm the connection. Each platform gets a unique bitrate and resolution, consuming significant upload bandwidth.

## Castr Overview

Castr is a professional multistreaming platform emphasizing feature depth and performance. Pricing starts around $500 annually and scales up significantly for larger streaming budgets. A free tier is not available.

The feature set is deeper than Restream. Castr provides advanced encoding options, backup failover systems, geographically distributed delivery, and sophisticated analytics. For professional streaming operations, these features matter.

The performance is optimized. Castr's infrastructure is designed to handle simultaneous streaming to many platforms with minimal quality degradation. If upload bandwidth is limited, Castr's encoding optimization can work around constraints better than alternatives.

The platform emphasizes reliability. Castr's backup encoding and failover systems mean if one platform connection fails, others continue streaming. For mission-critical services, this redundancy is valuable.

The interface is more complex than Restream. Castr assumes more technical expertise. Setup requires understanding encoding settings, bitrate management, and platform-specific configurations. For churches new to multistreaming, the learning curve is steeper.

The community documentation is smaller than Restream's. While Castr provides professional resources, the peer-to-peer documentation in church forums and YouTube is less abundant. You might need to work through problems independently or contact support directly.

## Head-to-Head: Accessibility vs Professional Features

This comparison reflects different target audiences. Restream optimizes for accessibility and ease-of-use. The setup is simple, the community documentation is abundant, and the free tier lets churches explore multistreaming without cost commitment.

Castr optimizes for professional features and performance. The interface is more complex but offers more control. The infrastructure is designed to handle demanding streaming scenarios. The reliability features and encoding optimization come at the cost of increased complexity.

For churches with standard upload bandwidth and straightforward multistreaming needs, Restream's simplicity is appealing. For churches with limited bandwidth, complex streaming requirements, or mission-critical reliability needs, Castr's advanced features justify the premium.

## Which Should Your Church Choose?

**Choose Restream** if your church is exploring multistreaming for the first time. The free tier lets you experiment without cost. If multistreaming becomes permanent, the free tier is adequate for basic simultaneous streaming.

**Choose Restream** if bandwidth is adequate. If your church internet can handle simultaneous streaming to multiple platforms, Restream is simpler and cheaper than Castr.

**Choose Restream** if learning from peer documentation matters. The abundance of YouTube tutorials and forum discussions specifically addressing church multistreaming makes learning easier.

**Choose Restream** if budget is the primary constraint. The free tier is usable, and paid tiers are reasonable. For most churches, Restream's pricing is accessible.

**Choose Castr** if your church has limited upload bandwidth and needs encoding optimization. Castr's advanced encoding can work around bandwidth constraints better than Restream.

**Choose Castr** if reliability is critical. The backup failover and redundancy features mean streaming continues even if one component fails. For churches where service interruption is highly undesirable, this matters.

**Choose Castr** if your streaming needs are complex. Multiple simultaneous services, geographically distributed viewers, or sophisticated platform-specific optimization make Castr's advanced features valuable.

**Choose Castr** if your church has dedicated streaming staff and can manage the more complex interface. For professional streaming operations, Castr's depth enables more sophisticated production.

**For most churches**, Restream is the starting point. The free tier is genuine, the learning curve is gentle, and the community documentation is abundant. Upgrade to Castr only when Restream's limitations become problematic.
