Small churches often face a specific mixer challenge: you need digital mixing capability without the space requirements or complexity of large-format consoles. The Allen & Heath CQ-18T and Behringer XR18 both address this niche, but they do so with different philosophies. The CQ-18T costs around $2000 and emphasizes an integrated touchscreen interface. The XR18 costs approximately $1200 and emphasizes app-based remote control. For small churches evaluating which compact mixer makes sense, this comparison matters.

## Allen & Heath CQ-18T Overview

The Allen & Heath CQ-18T is a compact mixer with a built-in touchscreen for control. Rather than operating remotely through an app, you operate directly from the mixer's integrated interface. The touchscreen provides immediate visual feedback and touch-based control that feels responsive and intuitive.

The build quality is excellent. Allen & Heath clearly designed this mixer as professional equipment. The materials are quality, the construction feels solid, and the overall design reflects careful engineering. This is equipment that will serve your church for years without reliability concerns.

The eighteen channels are adequate for most small worship services. The preamps are quality. The effects processing is comprehensive. From a pure features perspective, the CQ-18T offers everything a small church needs.

The interface is the centerpiece of its design. The touchscreen makes mixing feel immediate and responsive. You see the channels visually, tap to adjust levels, and watch the mixer respond instantly. This directness appeals to sound engineers accustomed to traditional mixing consoles — it feels more like mixing and less like navigating software.

The community is smaller than Behringer's. However, Allen & Heath provides excellent documentation and support. You're not left struggling to learn the mixer — the manufacturer has invested in thorough guides.

The trade-off is flexibility. The integrated touchscreen is powerful but less flexible than app-based control. You're operating from the mixer location, not roaming the sanctuary with a tablet. This limits the mobility advantage of networked mixing.

## Behringer X-Air XR18 Overview

The Behringer XR18 emphasizes app-based remote control. The physical mixer is compact and serves as the audio processor hub, with actual mixing operation happening through apps on iPad, laptop, or phone. This remote-first philosophy offers operational flexibility the CQ-18T doesn't provide.

The price is significantly lower — $1200 versus $2000. This $800 savings can be redirected to microphones, speakers, or other equipment that impacts worship experience.

The community is enormous. Thousands of churches use the XR18, sharing configurations, troubleshooting tips, and best practices constantly. If you have a question, you'll find answers quickly. YouTube tutorials cover nearly every scenario. Facebook groups are active and helpful. This peer support is genuinely valuable for churches new to digital mixing.

The remote control capability is powerful. Your sound engineer can move freely through the sanctuary during service, adjusting monitors and solving problems from anywhere on the network. For complex services or multiple leaders, this mobility is valuable.

However, mixing from an app requires getting comfortable without physical faders. The touchscreen and app interface are functional but less immediate than traditional console operation. Some experienced audio engineers find this less satisfying than hands-on fader control.

## Head-to-Head: Integrated Touchscreen vs App-Based Flexibility

This comparison reflects different operational priorities. The CQ-18T prioritizes immediate, tactile mixing from a dedicated interface. If you're mixing primarily from the console itself, the touchscreen feels natural and responsive. The directness of pressing buttons and seeing immediate feedback is satisfying.

The XR18 prioritizes flexibility and mobility. You're not tied to the mixer location. You can operate from the sanctuary, hearing what's happening in real-time and adjusting monitors or the mix without being physically at the equipment. For dynamic services with roaming speakers, this freedom is genuinely valuable.

The price difference is also substantial. At $1200, the XR18 costs less than the CQ-18T's $2000, and that savings is meaningful for budget-conscious churches.

## Which Should Your Church Choose?

**Choose Allen & Heath CQ-18T** if you prefer operating from a dedicated mixer with physical controls. If your sound engineer values the tactile experience of mixing with immediate visual feedback, the CQ-18T's touchscreen interface is noticeably more satisfying than mixing through an app.

**Choose CQ-18T** for professional build quality and long-term reliability. Allen & Heath equipment is built for professional use and survives decades of reliable operation. If you're thinking about ownership beyond ten years, the build quality justifies the investment.

**Choose CQ-18T** if your sound engineer will be mixing primarily from the mixer itself. If they're not comfortable mixing from a tablet or laptop, the integrated interface is the better choice.

**Choose Behringer XR18** if budget is the primary constraint. At $1200, you save $800 while getting capable digital mixing. This savings can transform your church's ability to invest in other production areas.

**Choose XR18** if mobility is important. If your sound engineer needs to roam the sanctuary during service for monitoring control or problem-solving, the app-based approach is liberating. They can adjust the mix from anywhere on the network.

**Choose XR18** if community support matters. The large XR18 community means you'll find answers quickly when you have questions. Facebook groups, YouTube tutorials, and peer configurations are abundant and helpful.

**Choose XR18** for volunteer sound engineers learning digital mixing. The abundance of online resources means new operators can find tutorials, answers, and configurations quickly. The learning curve is gentler with this community support.

**For small churches with experienced audio professionals**, the CQ-18T is an excellent choice if budget allows. The touchscreen interface and professional build quality deliver satisfaction and reliability.

**For small churches with limited budgets and volunteer sound operators**, the XR18 is the clear choice. The $800 savings and extensive community support make it the pragmatic decision.
