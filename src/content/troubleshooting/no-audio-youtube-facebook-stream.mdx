Your stream is live and viewers see video, but there's complete silence. The audio isn't reaching the platform. This is usually a misconfigured audio source, wrong track selection, or copyright muting — all fixable in under a minute. Let's get the sound back.

<ProductCTA variant="inline" />

## Step 1: Verify Audio Is Actually Being Captured in OBS

First, check that OBS is even *capturing* audio:

1. In OBS, look at the **Audio Mixer** at the bottom of the screen
2. Play some audio (talk into a mic, or play music from the mixer)
3. Watch the audio meters in OBS — do they show levels moving up and down?
4. If meters are **completely flat** (no movement), OBS isn't capturing audio at all — go to step 2
5. If meters are **moving**, OBS is capturing audio — skip to step 3

## Step 2: Add or Select the Correct Audio Source

If OBS isn't capturing audio, you need to add an audio input:

1. In OBS, go to **Settings > Audio**
2. Look at **Microphone** and **Desktop Audio** dropdowns
3. **Microphone**: select your audio interface, USB mixer, or microphone
4. **Desktop Audio**: select your mixer's USB output (if it has one)
5. Click **Apply** and then **OK**
6. Now check the mixer again — do you see audio levels?

If you still don't see audio levels, try selecting a different device in the dropdown (sometimes the wrong device is selected by default).

## Step 3: Verify Audio Tracks Are Enabled for Streaming

OBS can route audio to multiple places. Make sure audio is going to the stream output:

1. In OBS, go **Settings > Output > Streaming**
2. Look for **Audio Track** setting (or **Audio Bitrate**)
3. Verify **Audio Track 1** is selected (or whichever track you're using)
4. If nothing is selected, select **Audio Track 1**
5. Click **Apply** and **OK**

Without this setting, the stream output has no audio data.

## Step 4: Check That No Audio Devices Are Muted

Sometimes audio gets muted without you realizing:

**In OBS:**
1. Look at the **Audio Mixer** panel at the bottom
2. Check each audio source (microphone, desktop audio)
3. Verify the **mute button** (M icon) is NOT lit/pressed
4. If muted, click it to unmute

**In Windows:**
1. Right-click the speaker icon in the system tray (bottom right)
2. Click **Open Volume mixer**
3. Look for OBS Studio or your audio application
4. Verify the volume slider is not at zero
5. Verify there's no mute icon next to it

**In your mixer:**
1. If audio comes from a USB mixer (Behringer X32, Yamaha TF1, etc.), check that the **USB channel isn't muted** on the mixer itself
2. Look for a mute button on the USB channel and make sure it's not active

## Step 5: Test the Stream Audio with a Private Stream

Before going live publicly:

1. In OBS, click **Start Streaming**
2. On YouTube or Facebook, set the stream to **Private** or **Unlisted** (not public yet)
3. Open a second browser window and watch the stream
4. Do you hear audio? If yes, you're golden — make the stream public now
5. If still silent, continue to step 6

## Step 6: Check for Copyright-Detected Muting

If you're streaming worship music, YouTube or Facebook may automatically mute the audio due to copyright detection:

1. Check YouTube Studio or Facebook stream settings for warnings about copyright or music
2. If there's a copyright flag, the audio may be muted for that segment
3. Either:
   - Avoid the copyrighted content during the stream
   - Accept the copyright claim and let the platform monetize it (you won't earn revenue on that segment, but viewers will hear it)
   - Disable "automatic copyright detection" if you have permission to use the music (less common)

**Note:** Most churches have CCLI licenses that allow them to play worship music during services, but the license doesn't automatically cover streaming. Check with your CCLI provider about streaming rights.

## Step 7: Verify Your Mixer Channel Is Routed to the Stream Mix

If audio comes from a physical mixer:

1. Check that the channel sending audio to the PC is **enabled**
2. Verify the **channel fader** is not at zero
3. Check the **master output** fader is not at zero
4. If using a **USB output** on the mixer, verify it's set to output the correct mix (usually "Main Mix" or a dedicated "USB Mix")
5. Test by playing audio through the mixer and watching the PC's audio levels

## Audio Levels Are Clipping or Too Quiet?

If audio is present but the levels are wrong:

**Too quiet:**
- Increase the volume on your mixer's main output or USB send
- In OBS, increase the audio source volume (click the audio source > Filters > Volume Gain)

**Clipping/distorted:**
- Lower the volume on your mixer's main output
- In OBS, reduce the audio source volume in Filters
