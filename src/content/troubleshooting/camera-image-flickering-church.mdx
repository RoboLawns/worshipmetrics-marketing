Your camera feed is live, but the video flickers, stutters, or drops frames intermittently. Viewers are seeing a jumpy image, and it's distracting during the service. This usually points to one of four issues: power frequency mismatch, cable degradation, network packet loss, or frame rate mismatch. Let's fix it systematically.

<ProductCTA variant="inline" />

## Check Power Frequency (50Hz vs 60Hz)

Flickering that appears as a regular pulsing (especially under fluorescent or LED lights) is almost always a power frequency mismatch. Cameras have an anti-flicker setting that must match your country's electrical frequency:

1. Access the camera's web interface (navigate to `http://[CAMERA_IP]`)
2. Go to **Image Settings** or **Video Settings**
3. Look for **Power Frequency**, **Flicker Control**, or **Anti-Flicker**
4. If you're in the **US, Canada, or Japan**: set it to **60Hz**
5. If you're in **Europe, Australia, or most of the world**: set it to **50Hz**
6. Save and restart the camera
7. Check the live video — flickering should disappear almost immediately

If you're unsure which setting to use, try the 60Hz option first. If flickering persists, switch to 50Hz.

## Inspect the HDMI Cable (If Using HDMI Output)

If the camera outputs via HDMI to your switcher or capture card:

- **Replace the cable**: Use a high-quality HDMI 2.0 cable, not a cheap or old one. Bad cables cause intermittent signal dropouts that look like stuttering.
- **Check cable length**: HDMI has a practical limit of about 25 meters without a repeater. If your cable is longer, use an **HDMI repeater/booster** or switch to **SDI** (if your camera supports it).
- **Verify both ends are fully seated**: Push the HDMI connectors firmly into both the camera and the receiving device. Loose connections cause intermittent dropout.
- **Test with a different cable**: If you have a spare HDMI cable, temporarily swap it in to rule out cable failure.

## For NDI Cameras: Check Network Packet Loss

If your camera is streaming via NDI (not HDMI):

1. On the streaming PC, open **Command Prompt** (Windows) or **Terminal** (Mac)
2. Type `ping [CAMERA_IP]` and let it run for 30 seconds
3. Look at the results: you should see **0% packet loss**
4. If you see **any packet loss** (even 1–2%), the network is congested or unstable:
   - Move the streaming PC closer to the network switch or use a wired (Ethernet) connection if it's currently on Wi-Fi
   - Check if other devices on the network are downloading large files (ask staff to pause updates during the service)
   - If the camera is on Wi-Fi, move it closer to the router or reconfigure it to use a wired Ethernet connection
5. If packet loss persists, your network may be undersized — contact your IT staff about upgrading the switch or separating the AV network onto a dedicated VLAN

## Verify Frame Rate Matching

Mismatched frame rates between the camera and encoder can cause stuttering:

1. Check the camera's output frame rate (usually in Image Settings): is it **25fps** (PAL/50Hz) or **30fps** (NTSC/60Hz)?
2. In OBS, check **Settings > Video > Common FPS Values** and make sure it matches the camera
3. In vMix, check **Settings > Video > Video Mix 1** and match the frame rate
4. In your ATEM Mini or other hardware switcher, verify the project frame rate matches
5. All sources and the output must use the same frame rate, or you'll see stuttering

**Pro tip:** If you're in a 60Hz country, use **29.97fps** or **30fps** (not 25fps). If you're in a 50Hz country, use **25fps** (not 30fps).

## Check the Camera Power Supply

Unstable power can cause video glitches:

- Verify the camera is plugged into a **dedicated power outlet**, not a shared outlet with other high-power devices (projectors, lights, etc.)
- If using a power extension cord, make sure it's **rated for the camera's power draw** (check the camera's specs — typically 20–40W)
- Try plugging the camera directly into the wall outlet (not through a power strip) to rule out power conditioning issues
- If the camera has a backup battery or UPS option, ensure it's not in low-battery mode

## Restart the Camera and Streaming System

Sometimes firmware glitches cause stuttering. Perform a clean restart:

1. Power off the camera and wait 30 seconds
2. Close OBS or vMix completely
3. Restart your streaming PC
4. Power on the camera and wait for full boot (indicator lights stable)
5. Open OBS/vMix and start the stream
6. Monitor the first 2–3 minutes for flickering

---

**If flickering persists after these steps**, the issue may be lens auto-focus hunting (camera constantly refocusing). Access the camera's Image Settings and switch to **Manual Focus** or **Autofocus Lock**. Adjust focus once before the service starts and lock it.
