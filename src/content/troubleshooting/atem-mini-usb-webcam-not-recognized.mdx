## Step 1: Install the ATEM Mini Webcam Driver

ATEM webcam mode requires a driver:

1. Visit **blackmagicdesign.com/support**
2. Search for **ATEM Mini Drivers**
3. Download the **Webcam Driver** for your OS (Windows 10/11 or Mac)
4. Install the driver
5. Restart your PC or Mac
6. Plug the ATEM Mini into USB
7. Wait 10 seconds for Windows/Mac to recognize it

If you skip this step, the ATEM won't appear as a webcam.

## Step 2: Enable Webcam Mode on the ATEM

The ATEM has a setting to enable/disable webcam mode:

1. On the ATEM Mini, locate the **USB Mode** or **Webcam Mode** button or menu option
2. Verify **Webcam Mode** is **enabled**
3. Some ATEM models require you to go into **Settings > USB** and toggle it on
4. If you changed the setting, restart the ATEM

Without this enabled, the ATEM won't broadcast as a webcam.

## Step 3: Check Device Manager (Windows)

Verify Windows recognizes the ATEM as a webcam device:

1. Right-click **Start** and select **Device Manager**
2. Look for **Imaging devices** or **Camera** in the list
3. Expand it and look for "ATEM" or "Blackmagic"
4. If it appears without a warning icon, drivers are installed
5. If you see a **yellow warning icon**, right-click and select **Update driver** (or reinstall the driver from step 1)

## Step 4: Restart Your PC

Sometimes the OS doesn't immediately recognize the new device:

1. Connect the ATEM Mini to USB
2. Wait 5 seconds
3. Restart your PC
4. Once booted, open Zoom or Teams
5. Check if the ATEM now appears in the camera list

## Step 5: Test in OBS

OBS gives you good feedback on webcam detection:

1. Open OBS
2. Click **+** in Sources
3. Select **Video Capture Device**
4. In the **Device** dropdown, look for "ATEM Mini" or "Blackmagic"
5. If it appears, select it and click OK
6. You should see a preview in OBS

If the ATEM appears in OBS, it's recognized. If Zoom still doesn't see it, it may be a Zoom-specific setting.

## Step 6: Authorize Camera in Zoom/Teams

Even if Windows recognizes the camera, Zoom/Teams may need permission:

**Zoom:**
1. Open Zoom settings
2. Click **Audio/Video** or **Camera**
3. Look for **Camera** dropdown
4. Select **ATEM Mini** from the list
5. If not in the list, restart Zoom and try again

**Microsoft Teams:**
1. Open Teams settings
2. Click **Devices** or **Camera**
3. Select the ATEM Mini from the camera dropdown
4. If not listed, restart Teams

## Step 7: Check USB Webcam Version

Some older ATEM models don't support webcam mode:

- **ATEM Mini Pro:** Full webcam support
- **ATEM Mini Pro ISO:** Full webcam support
- **ATEM Mini:** Basic webcam support (confirm your model supports it)

If your model is very old, it may not have webcam drivers available. Check Blackmagic's support page to confirm your model supports webcam mode.

## Step 8: Use Different USB Port

Some USB ports work better than others:

1. Try plugging the ATEM into a different USB port (not a hub — direct to the PC)
2. Preferably a USB 3.0 port (faster, more compatible)
3. Wait 10 seconds and check if it appears in the device list

## Troubleshoot Zoom/Teams Not Showing Camera

Even if the ATEM appears in Windows, Zoom/Teams sometimes don't see it:

1. **Close and restart** Zoom/Teams completely (not just minimize)
2. Open **Device Manager** and verify the ATEM is still listed
3. If Zoom/Teams still don't see it, restart your PC
4. After restart, open the software and check the camera list again
