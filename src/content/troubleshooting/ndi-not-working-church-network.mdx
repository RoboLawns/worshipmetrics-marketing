Your NDI camera is on the network and broadcasting, but OBS and vMix don't see it. You can access the camera's web interface, so it's definitely online — NDI discovery just isn't working. This is almost always a network configuration issue: multicast is blocked, devices are on separate VLANs, or your switch isn't configured for mDNS. Let's restore NDI discovery.

<ProductCTA variant="inline" />

## Understanding NDI Discovery

Here's why NDI sometimes fails: **NDI uses multicast to announce and discover devices on the network.** Many consumer and business switches block multicast for performance reasons. If multicast is blocked, NDI devices can't find each other.

This is different from DHCP or regular IP routing — multicast is a special type of network traffic, and many network administrators disable it.

## Step 1: Verify the Camera Is Actually Broadcasting NDI

First, confirm the camera is set up to output NDI:

1. Access the camera's web interface (navigate to `http://[CAMERA_IP]`)
2. Look for **Network** or **Streaming Settings**
3. Find the **NDI** option
4. Verify it's **enabled** (not disabled)
5. Note the **NDI stream name** if there is one
6. Save and reboot the camera if you made any changes

If NDI is disabled, enable it and reboot the camera.

## Step 2: Check if Your Streaming PC and Camera Are on the Same Subnet

NDI discovery only works on the same local subnet:

1. **Find the camera's IP:** Go to the camera's web interface and note the IP (e.g., 192.168.1.100)
2. **Find your PC's IP:** On your streaming PC, open Command Prompt (Windows) or Terminal (Mac) and type `ipconfig` (Windows) or `ifconfig` (Mac)
3. Look at your network adapter and find the IP address
4. **Compare the first three octets:**
   - Camera: 192.168.1.100
   - PC: 192.168.1.50
   - Match? Good, same subnet.
   - Camera: 192.168.1.100
   - PC: 192.168.0.50
   - Don't match? Different subnets — skip to NDI Bridge (step 6)

If they're on the same subnet, continue to step 3.

## Step 3: Test with NDI Monitor

Download and use **NDI Tools** (free from Newtek/NDI) to verify multicast is working:

1. Go to **ndi.tv** and download **NDI Tools** for your OS
2. Install and open **NDI Monitor** (included in NDI Tools)
3. Look for your camera in the left panel
4. If the camera appears, NDI discovery is working — your OBS/vMix setup has an issue (skip to OBS/vMix section below)
5. If the camera does NOT appear in NDI Monitor, multicast is blocked on your network (continue to step 4)

## Step 4: Check Your Network Switch's Multicast Settings

If NDI Monitor doesn't see the camera, your network switch is likely blocking multicast. You need to configure the switch:

**If you have a managed switch** (typically enterprise or business-grade):
1. Access the switch's web interface (usually something like `http://192.168.1.254` — check your documentation)
2. Log in with admin credentials
3. Look for **IGMP Snooping** settings or **Multicast Settings**
4. **Enable IGMP Snooping** (this allows multicast to work)
5. Look for **IGMP Snooping Querier** and enable it if available
6. Save settings and restart the switch
7. Wait 2–3 minutes and test NDI discovery again

**If you have a consumer/unmanaged switch** (typically small, no admin interface):
- Consumer switches often block multicast by default
- There's usually no way to enable it without replacing the switch
- Solution: Upgrade to a managed switch, or use NDI Bridge (see step 6)

**To verify your switch type:**
- Managed switch: has a web interface (IP address you can log into)
- Unmanaged switch: just has ports, no configuration interface

## Step 5: Verify VLAN Separation Isn't Blocking Multicast

If your church has a managed network with VLANs (separate virtual networks), NDI devices on different VLANs can't discover each other:

1. **Ask your IT staff:** Are the camera and streaming PC on different VLANs?
2. If yes, multicast doesn't cross VLANs — that's why NDI fails
3. **Two solutions:**
   - Move both devices to the same VLAN
   - Use NDI Bridge to tunnel discovery across VLANs (step 6)

## Step 6: Use NDI Bridge for Cross-Subnet or Cross-VLAN Discovery

If the camera and PC are on different subnets or VLANs, use **NDI Bridge**:

1. Download **NDI Tools** (from ndi.tv)
2. Install it on a PC that **can see the camera** (on the same subnet or VLAN as the camera)
3. Run **NDI Bridge** (included in NDI Tools)
4. NDI Bridge runs as a service and converts multicast discovery into a unicast tunnel
5. On your streaming PC, in OBS or vMix, the camera should now appear as a discoverable NDI source

**How it works:** NDI Bridge tunnels the camera's NDI announcements so they can be seen across subnets/VLANs, even if multicast is blocked.

## Step 7: Check Your Firewall

Firewalls can block NDI on certain ports:

**Windows Firewall:**
1. Open **Windows Defender Firewall with Advanced Security**
2. Click **Inbound Rules** in the left panel
3. Look for rules related to **multicast**, **port 5353**, or **port 5004**
4. If any rule is **blocking**, delete it or create an exception
5. Add a new inbound rule allowing **UDP 5353 and 5004** for your streaming software

**Third-party firewalls:**
- Temporarily disable it to test
- If NDI works with firewall off, whitelist OBS or vMix in your firewall settings

**Hardware firewall (on your router):**
- Disable **multicast blocking** if your router has this option
- Access your router's admin panel and look for multicast or IGMP settings
- Enable multicast if disabled

## Step 8: Verify OBS/vMix NDI Configuration

Once multicast/NDI Bridge is set up:

**In OBS:**
1. Go to **Settings > Output > Streaming** (or wherever your NDI sources are)
2. Verify the **obs-ndi plugin is installed** and enabled
3. Click **Tools > obs-ndi Settings**
4. Check that NDI discovery is enabled
5. In your source, click **Scan for Sources**
6. Your camera should appear

**In vMix:**
1. Go to **Settings > Network**
2. Verify **NDI** is enabled
3. Click **Add Input > NDI**
4. Your camera should appear in the list

## Step 9: Restart Everything

Sometimes NDI gets stuck. Do a clean restart:

1. Power off the camera
2. Close OBS or vMix
3. Restart your streaming PC
4. Power on the camera and wait for full boot
5. Reopen OBS or vMix
6. Try adding the NDI source again

## Step 10: Test NDI Discovery

Before the service:

1. Run NDI Monitor and confirm your camera appears
2. In OBS/vMix, add the camera as an NDI source
3. Verify video appears in the preview
4. Save your scene
5. Do a 5-minute test stream

---

**Quick NDI troubleshooting checklist:**
- [ ] Camera NDI output is enabled in its web UI
- [ ] Camera and PC are on same subnet (or NDI Bridge is configured)
- [ ] NDI Monitor shows the camera as discoverable
- [ ] Switch has IGMP Snooping enabled (if managed switch)
- [ ] Not on separate VLANs (or NDI Bridge is handling it)
- [ ] Firewall not blocking UDP 5353/5004
- [ ] OBS-NDI plugin installed and working
- [ ] Test stream confirmed NDI source is visible and working
