# Behringer X32 Compact Setup Guide for Churches

The Behringer X32 Compact is a brilliant solution for smaller churches, remote campuses, or situations where space is at a premium but full mixing power is essential. It's the smaller sibling to the full X32, with identical software engine and most of the same professional capabilities, but with a more compact physical footprint and streamlined hardware layout. If your sanctuary is modest in size or your tech booth has limited space, the Compact might be the perfect fit.

## Key Differences Between Compact and Full X32

The X32 Compact shares the same core mixing engine as the full X32, meaning all the powerful features you know about—scenes, effects, routing, remote control—work identically. The differences are mostly about the physical layout and channel count. The full X32 has 32 faders arranged in 8 groups of 4 across a wide console. The Compact has 16 faders arranged in 4 groups of 4, and a control section that lets you access additional channels through banking.

Where the full X32 has dedicated physical outputs for 6 buses, the Compact has just 6 stereo outputs total—your main L/R, plus four additional stereo outputs for monitors or auxiliary sends. The Compact also has a slightly smaller mixer screen, though it displays the same information and is just as readable from across a small venue.

The rear panel of the Compact looks similar to the full X32 but is more compact. You have 16 XLR microphone inputs on the rear, which covers most small church setups. The full X32 gives you all 32 channels on the rear, but for churches with 8-12 active microphones and instruments, the Compact's 16 inputs are more than sufficient. If you need more than 16 inputs, additional channels can be added via Dante networking or other expansion options.

## Powering On and Initial Configuration

Connect the Compact to power and flip the power switch on the rear panel. After 10-15 seconds, the screen boots up showing the main mixing interface. Like all X32 products, confirm the sample rate is set to 48 kHz by going to Setup > Audio.

The Compact's smaller screen means navigation is slightly different. Instead of having all 32 channel faders visible at once, you see 16 faders. There are bank buttons allowing you to switch between different groups of channels—Channels 1-16 on one bank, Channels 17-32 on another bank. In practice, churches rarely need more than 16 channels, so most of the time you're on the first bank and never need to navigate elsewhere.

If you do use more than 16 channels (perhaps you have separate inputs for left and right piano, separate microphones for each worship leader, backup channels for redundancy), banking is simple. Just press the arrow buttons or bank select button to switch to the next set of channels.

## Optimal Input Configuration for Small Churches

Most churches with the Compact use a straightforward setup. Channels 1-2 are your primary and secondary vocal microphones. Channels 3-6 handle drums (kick, snare, toms, or sometimes a single stereo drum mix). Channels 7-8 are guitars. Channels 9-10 might be keyboards or a stereo mix from a keyboard. Channels 11-16 are available for additional instruments, a click track input, or other sources.

This leaves Channels 17-32 available but unused, which is fine. The X32 Compact's benefit is that you have them available if you need them, but you don't pay for 32 physical faders and the associated physical space if you don't.

For the rear panel connections, you'll use the XLR inputs on the Compact. The first 8 channels are clearly marked. You can also use the six 1/4-inch TRS inputs if you have line-level sources like keyboards or audio interfaces. These typically feed into channels 25-30.

## Audio Output Configuration

The Compact has a main stereo output (L/R) that goes to your power amplifier and main speakers. It has four additional stereo outputs. Most small churches use these for: one pair for stage monitors, one pair for an overflow room or recording feed, and two pairs reserved for future use.

To get sound to your main speakers, connect the Compact's main L/R output to your power amplifier using balanced XLR cables. Just as with the full X32, the physical volume in the room is controlled by the amplifier's gain, not primarily by the Compact's main faders.

## Introduction to Mix Matrices

The X32 Compact includes a mix matrix—a routing tool that allows you to combine multiple input channels into a custom mix and send that mix to one of the stereo outputs. This is more powerful than simple auxiliary buses because it allows complex, customizable mixing arrangements.

For most small churches, you don't need to dive deep into the mix matrix immediately. Your basic setup works with the main output and perhaps one auxiliary for monitors. However, understanding mix matrix exists is helpful for future expansion.

To access the mix matrix on the Compact, press the Mix Matrix button (or navigate through the menu to find it, depending on your firmware version). Here you can create custom stereo mixes by selecting which input channels and groups feed into each output. For example, you might create a drummer-only mix (just drums and click track) to send to the drummer's monitor, while a different mix goes to the worship leader.

## Scene Workflow for the Compact

The Compact's scene functionality works identically to the full X32. You save scenes with all your settings—channel levels, names, EQ, effects, routing—and recall them instantly. For a small church, a single "Sunday Service" scene is often sufficient. You set it up during a rehearsal, save it, and it's your default every Sunday.

As you grow comfortable, you might create separate scenes for different parts of your service—opening worship, announcements, sermon. The Compact handles this just as well as the full X32.

## Space-Saving Setup Tips

One advantage of the Compact is that it fits easily on a small desk or into a tight tech booth. Position it so you can comfortably reach the faders and see the screen. The channel faders are full-sized and feel professional despite the compact overall footprint.

Many churches place the Compact on a rolling cart with wheels so it can be moved between rooms or stored between services. The Compact weighs around 20 pounds, making it portable for multi-campus situations where you travel between buildings.

## Expanding Beyond the Compact's Limits

If your church grows and you consistently need more than 16 physical faders or more than 16 input channels, upgrading to the full X32 is straightforward. Your scenes, show files, and all your settings transfer directly from Compact to the full X32. You lose nothing. The full X32 simply gives you more physical channels and faders for more complex setups.

In the meantime, the X32 Compact is a fully capable, professional mixing console that serves small churches extraordinarily well. It's proven technology that's been successfully deployed in thousands of venues. Many churches start with the Compact and never need anything larger.

## Getting the Most from Your Compact

The Compact is capable of everything covered in our other X32 guides—streaming audio, remote control with X32-Edit, multi-track recording, proper gain staging, and scene-based workflows. The physical hardware is more compact, but the mixing engine is powerful.

Your first focus should be getting comfortable with basic mixing on the 16 faders you have. Learn gain staging, understand how scenes work, and build a reliable Sunday routine. Once those fundamentals are solid, explore the advanced features at your own pace. The Compact is built to grow with your needs.
