The Epiphan Webcaster X2 is designed for simplicity, but occasional connection issues arise. These are usually straightforward to diagnose and fix. This guide covers the most common problems and their solutions.

## No HDMI Signal Detected

You've connected your HDMI source to the Webcaster X2, but the web interface shows no video or reports "No HDMI Signal."

First, check that your HDMI cable is fully seated on both ends. Push the connector in firmly—even partially loose connections cause no-signal errors. If the cable seems connected, try disconnecting and reconnecting it.

Verify your video source is powered on and actively outputting video. If you're using a mixer or switcher, ensure it's powered on and the output you're connected to is active (not muted or faded out).

Try a different HDMI cable if one is available. Faulty cables are surprisingly common and look physically normal despite being internally damaged.

If you still see no signal after trying these steps, your video source might be outputting a resolution or format the Webcaster X2 doesn't support. Try a different video source (a backup camera, a laptop outputting HDMI) to test whether the problem is your primary video source.

## Network Connection Issues

The Webcaster X2 is connected via Ethernet but the web interface shows no internet connectivity, or streaming fails with connection errors.

First, verify your Ethernet cable is firmly plugged into both the Webcaster X2 and your network switch or router. A loose Ethernet connection causes intermittent or complete loss of network access.

Check whether other devices on the same network have internet access. Open a web browser on a computer and confirm you can reach a website. If other devices have internet but the Webcaster X2 doesn't, the device might not have received an IP address.

Access the Webcaster X2's network settings through its web interface (if you can still reach it locally at 192.168.1.100) and verify it has an IP address. If the IP address is missing or shows as 169.x.x.x, the device isn't communicating with your DHCP server.

Restart your network router. Sometimes routers become temporarily confused about devices on the network. Powering the router off for 30 seconds and back on resets these issues.

Restart the Webcaster X2. Unplug the power cable, wait 10 seconds, and plug it back in. Let it fully boot (approximately one minute), then check network connectivity again.

If you need the Webcaster X2 to use a static IP address rather than DHCP, manually assign one through the device's network settings. Use an IP in your network's subnet (for example, 192.168.1.50 if your router is 192.168.1.1).

## Streaming Fails with Authentication Error

You click **Start Streaming** but the Webcaster X2 immediately shows an authentication error. This means your stream key is invalid or expired.

Verify you copied your stream key correctly. Stream keys are long alphanumeric strings with no spaces. If even one character is wrong, authentication fails.

Log back into YouTube Creator Studio or Facebook and verify your stream key. Some platforms rotate keys periodically. Copy the current key and update the Webcaster X2's configuration. Test streaming again.

If you're certain your key is correct but authentication still fails, the platform might have reset your key. Try generating a new key from the platform (YouTube: "Regenerate"; Facebook: "Generate New Key"). Copy the new key into the Webcaster X2 and try streaming.

Sometimes platform servers take a few minutes to activate new keys. If you just generated a new key, wait five minutes before retrying.

## Streaming Starts but No Video Appears on Platform

The Webcaster X2 shows "Connected" status and your stream key validated, but when you check YouTube Creator Studio or Facebook, no video appears or the preview is black.

This usually indicates a signal format incompatibility. The resolution or frame rate your HDMI source outputs might be different from what the Webcaster X2 is configured for.

In the Webcaster X2's web interface, navigate to **Input Settings** or **Video Settings**. Check the detected resolution and frame rate. Verify these match your HDMI source's output.

If your mixer outputs 1080p30 but the Webcaster X2 is detecting 720p60, something is misconfigured. Go back to your video source and verify its output settings. Some mixers have output settings you can adjust.

After confirming the input settings match your source, restart streaming by stopping the current stream and starting a new one.

## Intermittent Video Glitches or Stuttering

You're streaming successfully, but video occasionally freezes, pixelates, or stutters. This usually indicates bandwidth issues—your upload connection can't sustain the configured bitrate.

Check your actual upload bandwidth using an online speed test. If your test shows 6 mbps upload but you're streaming at 6000 kbps (6 mbps), you're at maximum capacity with no headroom.

Reduce the Webcaster X2's bitrate to 4000-4500 kbps. This creates bandwidth headroom to handle normal fluctuations without stuttering.

Additionally, check what else is consuming bandwidth during streaming. If someone is downloading files, running backups, or streaming other content simultaneously, the Webcaster X2 won't have enough bandwidth. Coordinate network usage during services to avoid competition.

If glitches continue after reducing bitrate, your network connection might be fundamentally unstable. Check whether your router is in good condition, whether your Ethernet cable is high-quality, and whether your internet service is reliable. Consider contacting your ISP to test your connection quality.

## Cannot Access Web Interface

You've powered on the Webcaster X2 and connected it to Ethernet, but typing `webcaster-x2.local` in your browser doesn't load the web interface.

Try the default IP address `192.168.1.100` instead. If this works, your network's mDNS is disabled, but you can still access the device via IP address.

If the IP address doesn't work either, verify the Webcaster X2 has been powered on for at least two minutes to fully boot.

Check that your computer is on the same network as the Webcaster X2. If you're on Wi-Fi and the Webcaster X2 is on Ethernet, you should still be able to reach it if both are on the same network subnet.

As a last resort, restart your network router. Some routers have issues with device discovery on their first boot. Restarting the router sometimes resolves access issues.

## Firmware Update Issues

You've attempted to update the Webcaster X2's firmware, but the update failed or the device is stuck in a boot loop after the update.

Most firmware update issues resolve with a power cycle. Unplug the device, wait 30 seconds, and plug it back in. Let it boot fully (approximately 2-3 minutes for recovery from a failed update), then check whether normal operation resumes.

If the device remains unresponsive after a power cycle, the firmware update might have corrupted the device. This is extremely rare. Contact Epiphan support—they can provide recovery instructions or arrange a replacement if hardware is damaged.

## General Reliability

The Webcaster X2 is reliable and designed for simplicity. Most issues are resolved with the basic troubleshooting steps above. If you've tried these and the device still doesn't work, document what you've tried and contact Epiphan support.

Keep your firmware updated. Periodically check for updates in the web interface and install them during non-critical times. Updates improve stability and add features.

The Epiphan Webcaster X2 is an excellent choice for churches prioritizing simplicity. With basic troubleshooting, you'll get reliable service for years.
