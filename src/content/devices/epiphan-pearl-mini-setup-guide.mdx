The Epiphan Pearl Mini is a more advanced appliance than the Webcaster X2. Where the Webcaster X2 is purely for streaming to YouTube and Facebook, the Pearl Mini combines encoding, recording, and streaming into a single unified device. This is valuable for churches that want to stream their services for immediate public access while simultaneously recording high-quality files for archive and editing.

## Understanding the Pearl Mini's Role

The Pearl Mini accepts video inputs (HDMI and optionally additional sources) and simultaneously streams to platforms like YouTube/Facebook while recording to built-in storage. This eliminates the need for separate recording devices or backup encoders.

For example, your main service stream goes to YouTube for live public access, while the Pearl Mini also records the service in high quality to local storage. After the service, you can download the recording file and upload it to your archives, or edit it for highlight reels and promotional content.

## Physical Setup and Initial Configuration

The Pearl Mini arrives with the appliance unit, power supply, Ethernet cable, and documentation. It's larger than the Webcaster X2—about the size of a small audio interface—but still portable.

Connect power and plug in. Status lights illuminate. The device boots in 30-60 seconds. Connect the Ethernet cable to your network.

Access the web interface by typing `pearl-mini.local` in a browser, or use the default IP `192.168.1.100`. Set a password for configuration access.

## Connecting Video Sources

The Pearl Mini has multiple HDMI inputs (the exact number depends on your model). Connect your primary video source to the first HDMI input—typically your mixer or switcher output.

In the web interface, the Pearl Mini displays all connected inputs with video previews. Verify that your primary video source appears in the preview.

Some Pearl Mini configurations support additional input types beyond HDMI (like USB or external capture cards). Check your specific model's specifications.

## Configuring Streaming Destinations

Navigate to the **Streaming** section. The Pearl Mini allows you to configure streaming destinations just like the Webcaster X2—YouTube, Facebook, or custom RTMP destinations.

Add your YouTube stream key. Add your Facebook stream key. The process is identical to the Webcaster X2, but the Pearl Mini's interface is more feature-rich with additional options.

## Setting Up Local Recording

Navigate to the **Recording** section. This is where the Pearl Mini differentiates itself. You can configure recording quality, format, and storage location.

The Pearl Mini typically records to built-in storage (a hard drive or SSD inside the device). You can also configure it to record to external USB storage if available.

Set your recording quality. Some configurations offer options like:
- High quality recording (larger files, better preservation)
- Standard quality recording (moderate file size)
- Streaming-quality recording (smallest files)

For archive purposes, choose high-quality recording. You want your recorded files to be as high-quality as possible for future editing and re-release.

## Simultaneous Streaming and Recording

Configure both streaming destinations and recording parameters. The Pearl Mini simultaneously:
- Streams to YouTube at your configured quality
- Streams to Facebook at your configured quality
- Records to local storage at your configured quality

All three happen from the same HDMI input simultaneously. You're not limited to one activity per service.

The bandwidth consumption is primarily for streaming; recording doesn't consume network bandwidth since it's local to the device.

## Starting a Broadcast

Click **Start Recording** to begin recording to local storage. Click **Start Streaming** to begin streaming to your platforms. Both can be active simultaneously.

The web interface displays recording and streaming status. Monitor both to ensure everything is running as expected.

## Accessing Your Recorded Files

After your service, navigate to the **Files** or **Storage** section in the web interface. You'll see your recorded service file listed. You can download it to your computer through the web interface.

Downloaded files are typically in formats like MP4 or ProRes, depending on your recording configuration. These files can be:
- Uploaded directly to YouTube or your website for archival
- Edited in video editing software to create highlights or promotional content
- Shared with members who couldn't attend live
- Used for training or reference purposes

## Quality Settings and Bandwidth Considerations

The Pearl Mini's recording is independent of streaming bandwidth. You can record at very high quality (even 4K if your input supports it) while streaming at moderate quality for bandwidth-constrained situations.

For example, record at 1080p60 high-quality while streaming at 720p30 to YouTube and Facebook. Your recording is archive-quality, while your streams are efficient.

## Storage and Management

Built-in storage capacity varies by model, but typically provides 1-2 weeks of recording for a 2-hour weekly service at high quality. Monitor your storage regularly to avoid filling the drive.

Configure automatic deletion of old recordings if desired, or periodically download and backup your files to ensure you have archives.

Some churches use the Pearl Mini's recording capability as a backup. If streaming fails, you still have the local recording. You can upload it later.

## Integration with Multi-Input Scenarios

Some Pearl Mini configurations support multiple HDMI inputs simultaneously. You could have your main mixer input plus a camera input, and the Pearl Mini can display either or both in your streaming.

This advanced capability allows for multi-camera switching and more complex production setups than simple appliances typically support.

## Metadata and Advanced Options

The Pearl Mini includes advanced options for metadata—you can tag recordings with titles, descriptions, or custom fields. This helps organize your recorded files.

Some configurations include watermarking or overlay capabilities—you can burn your church's logo or service information into recordings.

## Archival Strategy

Over time, your Pearl Mini will accumulate many recorded services. Develop an archival strategy:
- Monthly backups of all recorded services to external hard drives
- Organized folder structure on your website or cloud storage
- Clear naming conventions (ChurchName_ServiceDate_Type)
- Regular review of which recordings you actually use

This prevents loss of valuable archives if the Pearl Mini's internal storage fails.

## Firmware and Updates

Periodically check for firmware updates in the web interface. Epiphan releases updates that improve performance, add features, and fix bugs. Install updates during non-critical times.

The Pearl Mini is Epiphan's professional-grade appliance for churches that want integrated recording and streaming. With proper configuration, it provides comprehensive broadcast and archive capability in a single device.
