# Mackie ProFX16v3 Setup for Small Church Streaming

The Mackie ProFX16v3 is a traditional analog mixer with integrated USB audio interface, perfect for small churches that want straightforward mixing without digital complexity, combined with the ability to stream services to online audiences. This guide walks you through initial setup and getting your audio flowing for both live PA and streaming.

## Understanding the ProFX16v3 Design

The ProFX16v3 is not a digital mixer—it's a classic analog console with built-in USB audio interface. All mixing happens through physical faders and switches. Your mix flows to your PA speakers and simultaneously via USB to your streaming computer. This analog simplicity combined with digital streaming capability makes it excellent for churches just beginning their technical journey.

The 16-channel capacity accommodates most small church worship setups. The built-in compression on channels provides basic dynamics control without requiring understanding of digital processing. The USB interface handles streaming without requiring separate audio devices.

## Connecting Your Audio Sources

The ProFX16v3 provides 16 channels of audio input via XLR and quarter-inch connectors. Connect your wireless microphone receivers to the first several channels using XLR cables. Connect your keyboard or other instruments to additional channels.

Establish clear, consistent organization: Lead vocal on channel 1, backup vocal on channel 2, keyboard on channel 3, acoustic guitar on channel 4, etc. This consistency helps you remember your setup and assists volunteers learning your mixing process.

## Setting Input Gains

For each input, activate the input and adjust the channel's input trim so that your signal sits in the optimal range. Most Mackie consoles use a green indicator light that should illuminate when the input is at proper gain level.

Speaking into microphones at normal service volume, adjust the trim until the channel's green light indicates proper level. This simple visual feedback takes moments to understand and ensures consistent, clean gain structure across all inputs.

## Routing Channels to Your Main Mix

The ProFX16v3 requires you to assign each channel to your main stereo output. Look for a button or switch on each channel labeled something like "Main Out" or a simple indicator showing which output the channel feeds.

Press this button on each channel you want in your main mix. Start with your critical channels: lead vocal, backup vocals, keyboard, and backing track. Don't include ambient room mics in the main mix initially.

## Setting Initial Mix Balance

Use the channel faders to set your initial mixing balance. Push your backing track fader to around -6 dB (a reference level for background music). Bring your lead vocal fader up until it sits prominently above the backing track, typically around -12 to -8 dB.

Adjust remaining instruments—keyboard around -12 dB, acoustic guitar around -15 dB, backup vocals slightly lower than lead vocals. These aren't absolute numbers; your specific sources and room determine ideal levels. However, this starting point provides a reasonable baseline.

Watch your main output meters as you balance. Aim for average levels in the green zone with occasional peaks in the yellow zone but never reaching red "clip" indicator. This prevents distortion while providing healthy signal to your PA system.

## Connecting USB to Your Streaming Computer

Locate the USB port on the ProFX16v3's rear panel. Connect a USB cable from this port to an available USB port on your streaming computer. The mixer should appear as a USB audio device on your computer within seconds.

If it doesn't appear, try a different USB port or restart both devices. Windows might require driver installation—check Mackie's website for the latest ProFX16v3 drivers if needed.

## Testing USB Audio Connection

In your computer's sound settings, verify that the Mackie ProFX16v3 appears in your audio input devices list. Open a simple audio recording application and test recording a few seconds of audio from the mixer while speaking into your microphones.

Play back the recording to verify audio quality is good and levels are appropriate. This simple test confirms that USB audio is working properly before you trust it with actual streaming.

## Configuring OBS for Mackie Audio

Open OBS Studio and navigate to Settings > Audio. In the Microphone dropdown, select the Mackie ProFX16v3. OBS will now receive audio from your mixer's USB output.

In the Audio Mixer panel at the bottom of OBS, ensure the input level is set appropriately. You want average levels showing around -18 dB with occasional peaks at -12 dB.

## Creating Your First OBS Scene

Create a new Scene in OBS. Add a Video Capture Device source if you have a camera, or a color background for audio-only streaming. The Mackie's audio is already configured globally, so you don't need to add audio sources individually.

Test this scene by doing a practice broadcast—even if just to a private YouTube stream. Let it run for a few minutes while your worship team practices normal music and speaking. Listen to how the audio sounds on the receiving end.

## Handling Compression and Effects

Each channel on the ProFX16v3 includes built-in compression. Use this on your vocal channels to smooth out performance variations. Compression helps ensure that quiet passages don't disappear and loud moments don't spike unexpectedly.

Set compression gently—you're not trying to heavily process the audio, just smooth out excessive dynamic range. A moderate setting usually works better than aggressive compression.

## Scene Documentation

While the ProFX16v3 doesn't have scene memory like digital consoles, create written documentation of your typical mixing setup. Note which channel gets which input, what the typical starting fader levels are, and any special instructions.

Print this documentation and keep it with your mixer. New operators can reference it quickly, accelerating their learning curve.

## Backup and Spare Parts

Keep spare USB cables and microphone cables on hand. USB cables occasionally fail, and having spares ensures you can maintain operations even if a cable becomes defective.

Also keep spare batteries for your wireless microphone systems if you use them. Running out of batteries during service is an easily preventable embarrassment.

## Expansion Possibilities

As your church grows, the ProFX16v3 might eventually feel limiting. When that time comes, you have clear upgrade paths to digital consoles like a Yamaha TF1 or Allen & Heath CQ-18T.

The ProFX16v3 doesn't lock you into a dead end. It's an excellent starting point that serves your church well for years while you develop your technical expertise.

## Maintenance and Care

The ProFX16v3 is mechanically robust. Periodically check that all cables remain properly connected. Occasionally wipe down the faders and switches if they accumulate dust or debris.

These simple practices keep your mixer in reliable working condition. The ProFX16v3, properly maintained, continues serving reliably for many years.

Your Mackie ProFX16v3 now provides straightforward analog mixing combined with streaming capability. This budget-friendly, simple-to-learn approach enables small churches to embrace digital streaming while maintaining familiar analog mixing workflows.
